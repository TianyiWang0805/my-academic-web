Research Interests: Smart Sensors, MEMS Devices, Micro/Nano Fabrication, Embedded Systems, Machine Learning for Gas Detection

Tianyi Wang is an undergraduate researcher at the East China University of Science and Technology (ECUST), majoring in Process Equipment and Control Engineering. With a GPA of 90.08/100 and ranked 1st out of 127 students, he has been awarded multiple top-tier scholarships, including the National Scholarship and the Yihai Kerry National Scholarship.

Tianyi’s research focuses on smart sensors, MEMS devices, and micro/nano fabrication technologies. He has led and contributed to interdisciplinary projects on hydrogel-based CO₂ sensors, ISFET pH sensors, and machine learning–enabled gas detection systems. His work has resulted in several academic papers, national patents, and recognition in high-level academic contests, such as the First Prize in the Challenge Cup National Competition and the International Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition.

Currently, he is a Mitacs Globalink Research Intern at the University of Windsor, fully funded by the Mitacs Globalink Scholarship. There, he is developing embedded hardware platforms for environmental sensing, integrating multi-sensor modules, PCB systems, and wireless communication protocols. His goal is to advance intelligent sensing systems that enable real-time, robust monitoring for environmental, biomedical, and industrial applications.

Poster Title
Design and Development of High-Performance Hydrogel Gas Sensors-The predecessor of my paper

Presented a poster on hydrogel-based CO₂ sensors at GIM & MES 2024, held on September 23, 2024, in Shanghai, China.
